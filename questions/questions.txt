Public Symmetry material points to a front-office, PM-facing rates role focused on **US rates**, especially **Treasuries, interest-rate swaps, futures, trading tools, analytics, and research/trade ideas**. Their published interview description also mentions an **aptitude assessment** and a technical conversation focused on **how you break down complex problems**. From my point of view, these are the questions most likely to come up.

## 1) Curve building

These are the most likely.

1. **How would you bootstrap a discount curve?**
   Start from liquid instruments, solve sequentially, explain why each new instrument pins down one new degree of freedom.

2. **Why do we use OIS discounting? Why did multi-curve become standard?**
   They will want the post-crisis intuition, not just buzzwords.

3. **How would you build a forward curve for 3M or 6M from futures/FRAs/swaps?**
   Be ready to discuss instrument selection by maturity bucket.

4. **Where would you interpolate — discount factors, zero rates, or forwards — and why?**
   This is a classic “good quant judgment” question.

5. **What can go wrong in a bootstrapped curve?**
   Negative forwards, kinks, instability, bad stubs, bad calendars, stale quotes, inconsistent inputs.

6. **How do you validate a curve build?**
   Reprice inputs, check smoothness, forward behavior, hedge stability, and day-on-day moves.

7. **If a single market quote is off-market, how do you detect and handle it?**
   They may push you on whether you drop it, smooth through it, or flag it.

8. **How would you incorporate futures into the front end of a SOFR curve?**
   You should be ready for futures-to-swap stitching issues.

## 2) Linear product pricing

Very likely.

9. **How do you price a vanilla fixed-for-floating IRS?**
   They will expect you to go from cashflows to discounting to par rate.

10. **Why is a par swap worth zero at inception?**
    Basic, but often asked to test clarity.

11. **How do you compute the forward swap rate?**
    Expect them to ask for both intuition and formula.

12. **How do you price a bond from a zero curve?**
    Then they may jump to yield, duration, convexity.

13. **What is the relationship between bond price and yield?**
    They may ask about convexity and why the relationship is nonlinear.

14. **How do Treasury futures work?**
    You should know conversion factors, CTD, invoice price, delivery option at a practical level.

15. **What is CTD and why can it change?**
    This is a very plausible question for a US-rates-focused seat.

16. **What is implied repo / cost of delivery intuition?**
    Current linear-rates quant mandates explicitly mention CoD/CTD-style detail. ([weareinvestigo.com][2])

## 3) Risk and hedging

Also highly likely.

17. **What is DV01 / PV01 / BPV?**
    Define it cleanly and say how you’d compute it.

18. **What is the difference between parallel DV01 and key-rate DV01?**
    They may ask why a hedge that is flat on total DV01 can still lose money.

19. **How would you hedge a swap with futures or Treasuries?**
    Watch for cheapest-to-deliver and bucket-matching discussion.

20. **What risks remain after a DV01-neutral hedge?**
    Curve, butterfly, basis, convexity, carry/roll, liquidity, funding, execution.

21. **How would you explain carry and rolldown on a rates trade?**
    This is a favorite PM-style question.

22. **What is a 2s10s steepener / flattening trade, and how do you size it risk-neutrally?**
    Be ready to hedge on DV01, not notionals.

23. **Why can a “hedged” butterfly still make or lose money?**
    This separates memorization from real understanding.

24. **How would you stress-test a rates book?**
    Parallel shocks, twists, butterflies, basis widening, funding shocks, event shocks.

25. **How would you do PnL attribution on a rates position?**
    Curve move, carry/roll, spread move, basis, residual.

## 4) Relative value / trading intuition

Given Symmetry’s public emphasis on fixed-income arbitrage and macro, I would expect at least some questions here. ([Startup Jobs][1])

26. **What makes a relative-value trade attractive in rates?**
    Cheap/rich, stable hedge, good carry, catalyst, manageable basis/liquidity risk.

27. **When do swap spreads widen or tighten?**
    They are not looking for one cause; they want a framework.

28. **How would you compare a Treasury trade versus a swap trade expressing the same rates view?**
    Funding, liquidity, balance sheet, carry, execution, basis.

29. **If you like the belly versus wings, how would you structure it?**
    Expect a butterfly answer with risk-neutral sizing.

30. **How do macro views feed into a linear-rates trade?**
    Growth/inflation/central-bank path → curve shape, front-end repricing, term premium.

## 5) Quant implementation / coding

Because the public role emphasizes tool-building, research automation, and technical problem solving, these are very plausible. ([Startup Jobs][1])

31. **Code a simple bootstrapper.**
    Even in pseudocode.

32. **Given cashflow dates and discount factors, compute swap PV.**

33. **How would you structure a rates analytics library?**
    Market data, conventions, calendars, instruments, curves, pricers, risk.

34. **What tests would you write for curve/pricing code?**
    Repricing tests, monotonicity/sanity checks, regression tests, convention tests.

35. **How do you handle bad data?**
    Missing quotes, stale quotes, bad rolls, holiday errors, outliers.

36. **How would you make the system fast enough for desk use?**
    Caching, incremental recalculation, vectorization, dependency graph, profiling.

38. **A trader says the model is wrong. What do you do first?**
39. **A hedge works historically but not in the last month. Why might that happen?**
40. **Describe a time you turned messy data into a usable trading signal.**
41. **How do you communicate technical disagreement with a PM or trader?**
42. **What would you automate first on a rates desk, and why?**

## The 8 questions I would bet on most

If you only have limited prep time, master these:

1. Bootstrap an OIS + forward curve
2. Why multi-curve and OIS discounting
3. Price a vanilla IRS and derive the par rate
4. DV01 / key-rate risk / hedge construction
5. Treasury futures, CTD, conversion factor
6. Carry + rolldown on a curve trade
7. Why a DV01-neutral trade still has risk
8. How you would debug a broken or unstable curve build

## What a strong answer sounds like

For this kind of seat, the best answers usually have **three layers**:

* instrument mechanics
* implementation detail
* trading/risk consequence

Example: don’t just say “interpolate zero rates.” Say **where**, **why**, **what pathology it avoids or creates**, and **how that choice affects hedge stability**.
